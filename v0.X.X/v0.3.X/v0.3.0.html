Mission Summary

You are stationed aboard the GSA Research Vessel Aurora, positioned at the edge of deep space far from electromagnetic interference. Your mission is to replicate the full sequence of cosmic evolution — beginning with the most fundamental particles of matter and building upward through every tier of physical and biological complexity until you have produced a self-sustaining living world.

The Aurora's Particle Supply Bench harvests elementary particles from the quantum vacuum and delivers them to your Crafting Island. Every synthesis you perform is a step in a chain that has taken the universe 13.8 billion years to complete naturally. You are compressing that history into a single mission.

Primary Objectives

① Nuclear Physics — Synthesise Protons and Neutrons from quarks and gluons. Assemble atomic nuclei.
② Atomic Chemistry — Surround nuclei with electrons to form Hydrogen, Carbon, Oxygen, and the other elements of life.
③ Molecular Biology — Bond atoms into Water, Glucose, Amino Acids, DNA, and the full toolkit of biochemistry.
④ Cellular Life — Assemble Prokaryote and Eukaryote cells. Build Proteins, DNA Strands, and Ribosomes.
⑤ Biosphere — Grow Plants and Animals. Build a complete self-sustaining Ecosystem. Synthesise a New World 🌍.

A PRACTICAL GUIDE TO SYNTHESISING MATTER
FROM QUARKS TO ECOSYSTEMS — GSA FIELD EDITION v4.1
Authored by Master Physicist Dr. Elara Venn, GSA Institute of Fundamental Sciences
Contributing: Prof. Idris Caelum · Dr. Sona Mirshad · Master Physicist T. Orren
TIER 0 — ELEMENTARY PARTICLES

These are the fundamental constituents of all matter. They cannot be synthesised — they emerge from the vacuum of space and are collected by the Supply Bench. Ensure the bench conduit is active before proceeding.
— Dr. Venn: "There is no shortcut below the quark. Patience is the first instrument of physics."

Up Quark & Down Quark T0 No recipe — supplied continuously by the bench. The Up quark carries charge +⅔; the Down carries −⅓. Three quarks in combination form a baryon. You will need large quantities of both.
Gluon T0 No recipe — supplied by bench. Gluons bind quarks together via the strong nuclear force. Without them, protons and neutrons cannot form. They are massless and cannot be directly observed.
Electron · Photon · Neutrino · W Boson T0 No recipe — supplied by bench at varying rates. Electrons are critical for every atom. Photons are needed for advanced synthesis (Plants). The W Boson governs beta decay. Prioritise electrons early.
TIER 1 — HADRONS & NUCLEI

Quarks bind under the strong force to form hadrons. Protons and neutrons are the stable baryons that make up all atomic nuclei. Prioritise these before attempting Tier 2.
— Prof. Caelum: "The proton is the workhorse of the universe. Cherish it."

Proton T1 Up Quark ×2 · Down Quark ×1 · Gluon ×1 Stable indefinitely. Forms the positive core of every atom. You will need many — hydrogen alone requires one per atom.
Neutron T1 Up Quark ×1 · Down Quark ×2 · Gluon ×1 Electrically neutral — it stabilises nuclei by mediating the strong force between protons. Heavier nuclei (Carbon, Oxygen) require more neutrons than protons.
Alpha Particle · Deuteron · Carbon Nucleus · Oxygen Nucleus · Magnesium Nucleus T1 Combinations of Protons and Neutrons. See Codex for exact counts. Intermediate nuclei used to build atoms. Magnesium Nucleus (12p+12n) is required for Chlorophyll synthesis. Plan ahead — it is expensive to assemble.
TIER 2 — ATOMS

Bind a nucleus to its electron shell and you have a complete atom. Hydrogen is the simplest; Carbon and Oxygen are the most strategically important — virtually every biological molecule downstream requires them.
— Dr. Mirshad: "Carbon is promiscuous. It bonds with almost anything. That is precisely why we love it."

Hydrogen (H) T2 Proton ×1 · Electron ×1 The most abundant atom in the universe. Required in enormous quantities for water, glucose, amino acids, and fatty acids. Build a large stockpile early.
Carbon (C) T2 Carbon Nucleus ×1 · Electron ×6 The backbone of all organic chemistry. Every biological molecule in Tiers 3–5 requires Carbon. Begin synthesis as early as possible.
Oxygen (O) · Nitrogen (N) · Phosphorus (P) T2 See Codex — each requires correct nucleus + electron count. Phosphorus (P-31: 15p+16n+15e) is essential for ATP and nucleotides — it is expensive but critical. Magnesium (Mg) is required for Chlorophyll. Nitrogen for all nitrogenous bases.
TIER 3 — MOLECULES

Chemistry begins here. Covalent bonds join atoms into the first functional structures. Water is the universal solvent; glucose is the universal fuel; amino acids are the universal building block.
— Master Physicist Orren: "Every ocean, every breath, every thought — Tier 3 made it possible."

Water (H₂O) T3 Hydrogen ×2 · Oxygen ×1 Required for Plants and Ecosystems. The bent geometry of the water molecule gives it unique polar properties — the reason life is possible at all.
Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) T3 Carbon Dioxide ×6 · Water ×6 · Photon ×6 Full Calvin cycle: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂. The primary energy currency of all cellular life. Requires Photons — ensure your supply is adequate.
Amino Acid · Fatty Acid · Nucleotide T3 Amino Acid: C×2,H×5,N×1,O×2 · Fatty Acid: C×4,H×8,O×2 · Nucleotide: Ribose+Nitrogenous Base+Phosphate Nucleotides now require Ribose (C₅H₁₀O₄), a Nitrogenous Base (adenine), and a Phosphate Group — the true building blocks of DNA and RNA.
Ribose · Nitrogenous Base · Phosphate Group · Hydrocarbon Chain T3 Ribose: C×5,H×10,O×4 · Nitrogenous Base: C×5,N×5,H×5 · Phosphate: P×1,O×4 · Hydrocarbon: C×3,H×8 New intermediate molecules. Hydrocarbon Chain is also needed for Chlorophyll's phytol tail. Build these in parallel with your nucleotide pipeline.
TIER 4 — CELLS & BIOLOGY

The transition from chemistry to life. Assemble all four structural components simultaneously: a lipid bilayer membrane, DNA encoding instructions, ribosomes to execute them, and ATP as energy. The result is the Prokaryote Cell — the first living entity.
— Dr. Venn: "When that first cell forms, do not underestimate the moment. You have crossed the boundary between physics and biology."

Protein → Ribosome → Prokaryote Cell T4 Core assembly chain — Amino Acid → Protein; RNA + Protein → Ribosome The ribosome is the factory that builds more proteins from RNA templates. It must itself be assembled from RNA and Protein — a classic bootstrapping problem.
DNA Strand · ATP T4 DNA: Nucleotide ×4 · ATP: Nucleotide ×1 + Phosphorus ×2 ATP is the cell's energy token — without it, no biochemical reaction runs. DNA carries the instruction set. Neither is optional.
Eukaryote Cell T4 Prokaryote Cell ×1 · DNA Strand ×2 Evolved from Prokaryote via endosymbiosis. Has a nucleus and organelles. Required for all Tier 5 organisms. The DNA cost represents the enlarged genome complexity.
TIER 5 — ORGANISMS & ECOSYSTEMS

The final tier. You are no longer working with particles — you are working with living things. A stable ecosystem requires both autotrophs (Plants) and heterotrophs (Animals) in balance, with water cycling through the system.
— Prof. Caelum: "The hard part is not making the first organism. The hard part is making the second."

Plant T5 Eukaryote Cell ×2 · Water ×2 · Carbon Dioxide ×1 · Photon ×4 Photosynthetic — converts light and CO₂ into glucose. The base of every food chain. Note the Photon requirement: ensure your supply bench has produced enough.
Animal T5 Eukaryote Cell ×2 · Glucose ×2 · Oxygen Gas ×1 · Protein ×2 Consumes Plants indirectly via glucose and oxygen. Maintains the carbon cycle that keeps the ecosystem stable.
Ecosystem → NEW WORLD 🌍 T5 Ecosystem: Plant + Animal + Water ×3 · New World: Ecosystem ×1 Mission complete. A self-sustaining biosphere capable of indefinite operation without external input. You have remade the world.
